Settings: Tenant

In an online health-care environment, a ‘Tenant’ is usually referred to as a specific department, unit, or a separate healthcare facility that uses the software. Each tenant has its own unique settings to tailor the software to its specific needs.

The ‘Settings’ feature comprises of five headings. They are:
i. Practice Information
ii. Appointment Settings
iii. Notification Settings
iv. PayPal Settings
v. Subscription Details
Let us look into each heading in detail.


i. Practice Information

Practice Information contains basic details such as Practice Name, Practice Type Code, First Name and Last Name (of point of contact), and so on. ‘Practice Type Code’ is a numerical or alphanumeric code that identifies the type of medical practice or healthcare facility.


ii. Appointment Settings

Appointments in a medical facility vary. Some of the common ones are primary care appointments, specialist appointments, diagnostic appointments, and vaccination appointments.

To edit a newly entered ‘Appointment Type’, click on the text field. The text field and font size within enlarges for easy readability. Input the new changes, and press Enter key to confirm.

Add as many as applicable. Click on the checkbox labelled ‘Run patient eligibility checks’ if you want a patient’s insurance to be screened before the start of his medical checkup.


iii. Notification Settings

Notifications (from a medical facility) sent to patients enhance their engagement with the healthcare management. Ensure that email addresses and phone numbers are entered correctly, and all relevant check boxes are selected.


iv. PayPal Settings

As with all IDs involving financial transactions, double check your Brain Tree ID, Key and Private Key. Use caution and treat all such IDs as sensitive information.


v. Subscription Details

Subscription details refer to information related to a subscription service or plan that a user or healthcare organization has opted for within the software. The checkbox marked ‘Skip identity verification’ is used to control the E-Prescription status verification of the Provider. If the box is left unchecked, the NPI of Provider will be validated at the time of On-boarding. The E-Prescription status will be enabled only after successful verification of NPI.
