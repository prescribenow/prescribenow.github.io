SOAP Note

A patient’s Subjective information (symptoms, feelings and concerns), Objective data (vitals and lab results), Assessment (physician’s diagnosis) and Plan (course of action to treat patient) make up a SOAP note. These notes are used extensively for monitoring a patient’s progress, physician-patient documentation, physician-physician collaboration, insurance and billing, etc.

On the Home tab, click on ‘Start’ (or ‘Resume’) under ‘Upcoming Appointments’. If the appointment type is follow-up, this will take you to a patient’s SOAP note.

A SOAP note has four parts.

1. This is a ‘Subjective’ section for a supposed patient. It contains ‘Chief Complaint’, ‘History of Present Illness’ and ‘Past Medical History’. It also contains ‘Review of Systems’ (not shown here).

2. The Objective section focuses on the measurable and observable data of a patient. These include:

i. Vital Signs consists of temperature, blood pressure, pulse, oxygen saturation level, height, weight and BMI. It must be kept in mind that Vital readings have a limited human range. For example, ‘Temp’ in Fahrenheit has a range of 90 to 105.

ii. Physical Examination Findings (general appearance, cardiovascular examination findings, gastrointestinal examination findings, etc.)

iii. Laboratory test results

iv. Objective data related to the patient's Chief Complaint
v. Objective data related to the patient's Past Medical History

3. The Assessment section provides a concise summary and analysis of the patient's medical condition. This includes a provider's professional assessment and interpretation of the subjective and objective information presented in the prior sections.

4. The Plan section outlines the proposed or prescribed course of action for a patient's medical care. It includes, among others:
i. Treatment Plan
ii. Referrals
iii. Patient Education
iv. Orders and Signature

5. Medication Adjustments

SOAP notes always carry patient details from previous patient-provider encounters. This helps the provider to better understand the patient’s ailment and take steps to treat it properly.

Using previously diagnosed data improves the accuracy of analyzing a patient's present condition. Be sure to select Patient Encounter from a previous date to display its Encounter.

Lastly, ensure that Primary Diagnosis (of patient) has been added before signing the SOAP note. Signing a SOAP Note is important for several reasons; some of which are:

I. Billing and Insurance Reimbursements
II. Care continuity
III. Patient Involvement
IV. Legal Documentation and Protection
V. Communication

There are other ways to initiate a SOAP Note:

  • Click on Patients > Desired Patient's name > New (top right corner) > SOAP Note

  • Click on Encounters > Specific Encounter Resume > (If Encounter is a follow-up) > SOAP Note

  • Home > Pending Encounters (on top) > Select Specific Encounters > (If Encounter is a follow-up) > SOAP Note

  • Click on Patients > Desired Patient’s name > Patient Information > Pending Encounters (on top) > (If Encounter is a follow-up) > SOAP Note

‘Appointment’ can also be scheduled on the patient’s portal by the patient and Intake Form link.
