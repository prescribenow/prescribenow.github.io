Booking Appointments

1. To make a new appointment, click on ‘Appointments’ from the left pane and select ‘New Appointment’ at the upper right-hand corner of your screen.

2. The above screen opens in front of you. Search for and add the patient’s name. Select ‘In-Person’ if patient will physically appear for a medical examination in the hospital. Select ‘Virtual’ if the patient and healthcare provider will connect through video conferencing to discuss medical concerns.

3. Select the facility under ‘Facilities’. If the appointment is ‘Virtual’, the ‘Facilities’ option will not appear. This is because a provider is not bound to attend to a virtual patient from any particular facility.

4. Choose your health care provider under ‘Provider’. If you select ‘Any’, time slots of all providers registered in the same state as the patient will be displayed.

5. ‘Appointment Type’ has three selectable options by default. ‘Consultation’ is chosen when a new patient is to be treated or a registered patient with a new illness needs to be seen by a physician. If you select ‘Follow Up Appointment’, another field titled ‘Past Appointments’ will appear. Select the past appointment to align with the upcoming follow up. ‘Custom Appointment’ is used in emergencies. It allows you to choose a physician who may not be scheduled. It also allows you to pick a time and duration (of appointment) of your choosing.

6. Under ‘Reason for Visit’, state the reason or reasons why this visit is necessary.

7. Consent form is a legal document that shows a patient’s agreement to have any type of medical procedure done. If any consent form is applicable for this appointment, search for and select it.

8. There are two ways to view available time slots. The general way is to book an appointment by ‘Date and Time’. The other way is to look for your physician’s earliest availability.

The default view is ‘Date and time’. Select the date and time of your choice.

If you select ‘Provider’s earliest availability’, you will be presented with ‘Date’ and ‘Start Time’ against the name of your health care provider. You can select the dates of your appointment. Selecting dates from the calendar icon will list time slots (of physicians) from that day onward. Clicking on the red arrows allows you to peruse all available days and time slots for your physician.

9. When done, click on ‘Schedule’ to confirm and schedule your appointment.

10. Speaking of time, it should be remembered that ‘Start Time’ is according to the patient’s own time zone.

11. Booked appointments can be seen by clicking on ‘Appointments’ from the home screen. A calendar of the current month opens to reveal all dates in which appointments have been made.

12. Clicking on any booked date highlights the appointment details to the right of the calendar. You can either click on an empty space within a booked date or the dark blue highlighted area. The ‘empty-space-click’ displays a short summary of the appointment. Patient’s name, gender, age, chief complaint (if any) and time of appointment are shown.

13. Selecting the three vertical dots displays a menu with two options; namely ‘Edit’ and ‘Cancel’.

14. “Edit’ allows you to make amendments in the booked appointment. When the required modifications have been made, select ‘Reschedule’ to confirm the changes. Please remember that name of patient cannot be changed.

15. If you select ‘Cancel’, a confirmation message appears. Selecting ‘Cancel Appointment’ deletes the appointment from the calendar. Please remember that past appointments cannot be cancelled.

16. While looking at the appointment calendar, if the dark blue highlighted area is clicked, a detailed appointment summary is seen.

Details include currently booked appointment time and date, name and address of facility (where provider-patient consultation will take place), patient allergies, recent diagnoses and active medications.

Another way to make a new appointment is by clicking on:

  • Patients > Specific Patient's name > New (top right corner) > Appointments

‘Appointment’ can also be scheduled on the patient’s portal by the patient and Intake Form link.
