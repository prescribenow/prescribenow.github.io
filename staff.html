Staff

When a Staff has logged in, a screen like the one shown below appears in front of the Staff:

Appointment time of patient, patient details, name of assigned ‘Provider’, ‘Exam Room’ and ‘Visit Status’ are some details visible here. ‘Exam Room’ is the provider examination room allocated to the patient. ‘Visit Status’ is the current status of the patient. Following are detailed sections present in this category:

i. Scheduled - The patient has an upcoming appointment that has been scheduled in advance.
ii. Check-in: The patient has arrived at the healthcare facility and is in the process of registering their presence for the scheduled appointment.
iii. MA Review: The patient's information and vital signs are being reviewed by a Medical Assistant before they see the healthcare provider. An MA gathers preliminary information such as weight, blood pressure, and reason for the visit.
iv. Provider Ready: The provider is ready to see the patient. The patient is in queue and the provider is prepared for the consultation.
v. In Consultation: The patient is currently meeting with the healthcare provider.

vi. Consultation Complete: The patient has finished the consultation with the healthcare provider. This includes medical discussion, examination, or treatment with the provider.
vii. Visit Complete: The entire visit, including the consultation and any additional procedures, is complete.
viii. No-show: The patient did not show up for the scheduled appointment.
ix. Canceled: The patient or the healthcare provider has canceled the scheduled appointment.

The Staff is also responsible to assign the patient a ‘Medical Assistant’, who will assist the patient with the vitals and other necessary information.

By clicking on the name of the patient, the Staff is directed to a patient information screen. At the bottom of the screen, the Staff assigns an examination room and a medical assistant to the patient and clicks on ‘Check-In’. This confirms the presence of the patient in the healthcare facility and alerts the medical assistant of the task ahead.

The provider is notified about the patient's presence, and status of the patient changes from ‘Scheduled’ to ‘Checked In’.
