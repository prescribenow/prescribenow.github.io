Patient Onboarding

'Patient Onboarding' means introducing a new patient to a healthcare facility's electronic systems and procedures.

To register a patient, select the following:

You will arrive at the tab titled ‘General’. This tab contains three parts. The first part allows you to input general information about the patient, such as name, date of birth and address. The second part is titled ‘Contact Information’, requiring you to input the patient’s mobile phone number, email and home phone number. ‘Emergency Contact Information’ is the third part of this tab. It contains information about someone who may be contacted during an emergency. Fill in the text fields with appropriate information. All text fields with an asterisk must be filled out as a requirement.

The second tab is for 'Insurance'. It contains primary, secondary and tertiary medical insurances according to the coverage they provide to a particular patient.

A patient is required to provide details such as the 'Payer Name,' 'Policy Number,' and 'Member ID.' Make sure to include the 'Effective' and 'Termination' dates for insurance, and clearly indicate the 'Relationship to Insured.'

By clicking the 'Save' button located at the bottom, you can store all the information entered. An invitation email link will be sent to the patient at the provided email address.

Upon clicking the link, patient will be redirected to the password setup page.

The patient will now be required to set and confirm the website password. By marking the provided checkbox, the patient will receive all website notifications, including emails, reminders, prescription orders, and lab results.

Patients who are less than the age of 12 are treated as minors. When prescribing their medication, please ensure that their height and weight are entered into the system. This allows correct dosage to be administered to them.
