Medical Assistant

The Medical Assistant’s prime obligation is to make the patient ‘Provider Ready’. This means that the patient’s vitals, medication history and previous lab reports are taken and set for the physician to view them and act accordingly.

When the Medical Assistant (MA) logs into the system, the patient’s name and details can be seen on the screen. MA responsibilities include a mix of administrative and clinical tasks. This allows a provider to focus on patient care. Examples of MA tasks are:

iv. Help physicians during physical examinations, procedures, and minor surgeries.
v. Inform patients about medication, treatment, and post-visit care instructions.

Once the patient is ready to be seen by the provider, the Medical Assistant sets the status ‘Provider Ready’ on the system.

The Provider can also view the patient’s status on their system. A banner at the top reminds the provider of the forthcoming appointment.

Another prompt is present under the heading ‘Upcoming Appointments’.

Once the patient has been seen, and the provider concludes the consultation, status of the patient turns to ‘Consultation Complete’. This appears in a grey shade.
